    Weapons & Countermeasures — Types, Mechanics & Limpet Tasks¶

    Overview¶

    Combat in the game revolves around four distinct weapon types, each with unique tactical roles and mechanical behaviors. Weapons are organized into groups of up to 8 configurable slots, allowing players to switch between different loadouts mid-flight. Every weapon generates heat during use, creating a risk-reward cycle where sustained fire must be balanced against overheating penalties.

    Defensive countermeasures operate independently from offensive weapons, providing automated or manual protection against incoming threats. Point defense systems intercept projectiles, while other countermeasure types (ECM, chaff) disrupt enemy targeting and sensor systems.

    Weapon Types¶

    Laser Weapons — Beam-Based Instant Hit¶

    Laser weapons fire continuous beams of concentrated energy that strike their target instantly upon firing. There is no travel time, making lasers the most accurate weapon type at any range — accuracy depends entirely on pilot skill in keeping the beam centered on the target.

    Tactical Role: Consistent damage output, effective against fast-moving targets, requires sustained aim for maximum effect.

    Property Default Description
    damage 5.0 Damage per tick while the beam is active (typically 0.2s intervals)
    ramp_time 1.5 Seconds to reach full power from cold start — early shots do reduced damage
    weapon_range 1,200 Maximum effective range; beams don't extend beyond this distance

    Visual Properties: | Property | Default | Description | |----------|---------|-------------| | beam_color | Red #FF3333 | Color of the laser beam visual | | beam_width | 0.05 | Thickness of the beam cylinder | | beam_style | "solid" | Rendering style (solid, pulsing, split) |

    Beam Effects: | Property | Default | Description | |----------|---------|-------------| | pulse_rate | 0.0 | Frequency of visual pulse waves traveling along the beam | | pulse_strength | 0.6 | Intensity of the pulse wave effect | | pulse_count | 8 | Number of simultaneous pulse waves on the beam | | pulse_speed | 6.0 | Speed at which pulses travel from muzzle to target | | beam_split_count | 1 | Number of parallel beams (for multi-barrel weapons) | | beam_split_separation | 0.05 | Lateral spacing between split beams |

    Heat Management: | Property | Default | Description | |----------|---------|-------------| | heat_capacity | 100.0 | Total heat units before overheating occurs | | heat_rate | 25.0 | Heat generated per second while firing | | heat_recharge | 20.0 | Heat dissipation rate when not firing (units/second) | | heat_recharge_delay | 1.0 | Seconds of cooling pause before heat starts dissipating |

    Overheat Mechanics: When a weapon's accumulated heat reaches its capacity, it enters an overheat state: - Firing is temporarily disabled until heat drops below the resume threshold (overheat_resume_threshold_pct, default 25%) - Heat dissipation slows significantly during overheat (overheat_recharge_multiplier, default 0.25× normal rate) - An optional enforced cooldown period may apply (overheat_cooldown, default 2 seconds)

    Charged Shots: Some lasers support holding fire to charge for a more powerful shot: | Property | Default | Description | |----------|---------|-------------| | charge_time_max | 3.0 | Maximum charge time in seconds | | charged_damage_multiplier | 3.0 | Damage multiplier for fully charged shots | | charged_cooldown_multiplier | 2.5 | Heat penalty multiplier after firing a charged shot |

    Gimbaled Mounting: Weapons can be mounted on gimbals that track targets independently of ship orientation: | Property | Default | Description | |----------|---------|-------------| | gimbaled | false | Whether the weapon tracks targets automatically | | gimbal_turn_rate | 6.0 | Maximum rotation speed for gimbaled tracking (degrees/second) |

    Gimbaled weapons are essential for fighting enemies that maneuver unpredictably, as they maintain aim without requiring the pilot to point their ship at the target.

    Plasma Weapons — Projectile-Based with Travel Time¶

    Plasma weapons fire glowing projectiles of superheated ionized gas that travel through space before impacting their target. The projectile's flight time creates a skill gap: skilled pilots can dodge plasma bolts by maneuvering, while unmaneuverable targets take guaranteed damage.

    Tactical Role: High single-hit damage, effective against slow or large targets, requires leading moving targets.

    Property Default Description
    speed 220.0 Projectile travel speed (game units/second) — faster = easier to hit
    damage 12.0 Damage dealt on impact with target or shield
    max_lifetime 6.0 Maximum flight time before the projectile self-destructs
    proximity_radius 5.0 Distance at which the projectile detonates near its target (not direct hit)

    Behavior: Plasma projectiles track their designated target using smooth interpolation (slerp) toward the target's current position, with a turn rate of approximately 6 radians/second. This gives them slight homing capability while maintaining visible flight paths that skilled pilots can dodge.

    When a plasma projectile enters the proximity radius of its target, it detonates immediately, dealing damage to anything within range. If it collides directly with a body or shield area, it also detonates on contact. The projectile ignores collisions with its source ship and all descendants (preventing self-damage).

    Rocket Weapons — Homing Missiles with Explosions¶

    Rocket weapons fire guided missiles that actively track their targets using proportional navigation. Rockets combine the homing capability of plasma projectiles with explosive warheads that create visible detonation effects on impact.

    Tactical Role: Area denial, lock-and-forget engagements, high damage with visual impact.

    Property Default Description
    speed 120.0 Missile cruise speed (slower than plasma but highly maneuverable)
    turn_rate 8.0 Maximum turning rate for homing guidance (radians/second) — higher = tighter turns
    damage 20.0 Damage dealt on impact or proximity detonation
    max_lifetime 6.0 Maximum flight time before fuel exhaustion and self-destruction
    proximity_radius 10.0 Distance at which the missile detonates near its target

    Explosion Effects: On impact, rockets spawn a visual explosion effect: | Property | Default | Description | |----------|---------|-------------| | explosion_color | Orange #FF9933 | Color of the explosion sphere | | explosion_size | 3.0 | Maximum scale multiplier for the explosion visual | | explosion_duration | 0.25 | Seconds for the explosion to expand and fade |

    Homing Behavior: Rockets use smooth directional interpolation (slerp) toward their target, with the turn rate clamped per-frame to prevent instant direction changes. This creates realistic missile flight paths that can be countered by countermeasures or extreme maneuvering.

    Rockets detect when they've lost their target (target nullified or out of range) and continue on their last known trajectory until fuel exhaustion or impact with any solid object. They also detonate on direct collision with any body or area that isn't part of the launching ship's hierarchy.

    Shot Down Capability: Rockets can be intercepted by point defense systems or other anti-missile countermeasures via the shot_down(source) method, which triggers an explosion at the rocket's current position without dealing damage to anything else.

    Probe Weapons — Utility Scanning Probes¶

    Probe weapons fire non-lethal scanning probes designed for reconnaissance, surveying, and data collection rather than combat. These probes follow their targets but emit a probe_hit signal instead of dealing damage, making them useful for discovery mechanics and environmental scanning.

    Tactical Role: Non-combat utility, target identification, exploration support.

    Property Default Description
    speed 120.0 Probe travel speed (game units/second)
    max_lifetime 6.0 Maximum flight time before the probe self-destructs
    proximity_hit_radius 3.0 Distance at which the probe registers a hit on its target

    Behavior: Probes use gentle homing with a turn rate of approximately 2 radians/second (much slower than rockets), creating slow, deliberate flight paths suitable for scanning operations. When a probe enters proximity range of its target or collides with any object, it emits a probe_hit(target) signal and self-destructs without dealing damage.

    The dynamic hit radius scales with speed and delta time (max(proximity_hit_radius, speed × delta × 1.25)) to prevent tunneling through small targets at high velocities. Probes ignore collisions with their source ship and all descendants.

    Countermeasures¶

    Countermeasure systems provide defensive capabilities against incoming threats. They are installed in dedicated countermeasure slots and can operate automatically or manually, depending on configuration.

    Point Defense Systems¶

    Point defense (PD) is the most common countermeasure type, providing automated interception of incoming projectiles and rockets. PD systems detect threats within their trigger range and fire back to destroy them before they reach the ship.

    Property Default Description
    countermeasure_type "point_defense" Classification identifying this as a point defense system
    auto_trigger true Whether the system activates automatically when threats are detected
    trigger_range 300.0 Maximum distance at which PD can detect and engage incoming projectiles
    cooldown 1.0 Seconds between PD activation cycles
    ammo_capacity 500 Total ammunition available before reloading is required
    ammo_per_shot 1 Ammunition consumed per interception attempt

    Point defense systems are particularly effective against rocket weapons due to the rockets' relatively slow speed and predictable flight paths. They are less effective against lasers (which travel instantly) and plasma projectiles (which travel faster than most PD reaction times).

    Enemy Point Defense Gating¶

    Enemy ships don't all carry point defense — it's gated by difficulty level and faction affiliation: | Property | Default | Description | |----------|---------|-------------| | point_defense_chance | 0.0 | Probability that an enemy ship carries PD (0–1 scale) | | point_defense_min_difficulty | 1 | Minimum difficulty level for PD availability | | point_defense_max_difficulty | 10 | Maximum difficulty level for PD availability | | point_defense_allowed_faction_ids | [] | Faction whitelist; empty = all factions can have PD |

    This means low-difficulty enemies may lack point defense entirely, while high-difficulty faction-specific enemies might only carry PD if they're on the allowed list.

    Weapon Groups¶

    Weapons are organized into groups of up to 8 configurable slots (WEAPON_GROUP_COUNT = 8). Each group contains one or more weapons that can be fired together or in sequence, and players can switch between groups during combat using weapon group switching mechanics.

    Fire Policies¶

    Each weapon group has a fire policy that determines how its weapons are discharged:

    Policy Code Description
    Simultaneous SIMULTANEOUS (0) All weapons in the group fire at the same time — maximum damage output but generates heat from all weapons simultaneously
    Sequential SEQUENTIAL (1) Weapons fire one after another in order — lower peak heat generation but reduced overall DPS

    Group Management¶

    Weapon groups are managed through ShipData methods: - get_weapon_group_weapons(index) — Returns the list of weapons in a group - set_weapon_group_weapons(index, weapons) — Assigns weapons to a group - get_active_weapon_data() — Returns the primary weapon from the currently active group - set_active_weapon_group(index) — Switches to a different weapon group

    The game tracks which groups are non-empty (get_non_empty_weapon_group_indices) and provides cycling through available groups (get_next_non_empty_group_index), allowing players to switch between loadouts using simple forward/backward navigation.

    Hardpoint Slots¶

    Weapons are physically mounted in hardpoint slots defined by ShipSlotDefinition resources with slot_type = HARDPOINT. Each hardpoint has an ID (e.g., "hardpoint_0", "hardpoint_1") and optional tags for filtering compatible weapons. The loadout system validates that only WeaponData instances are installed in hardpoint slots.
