    Factions — Identities, Governments, Relations & Lore¶

    Overview¶

    The political landscape of the game galaxy is shaped by seven major factions, each with distinct governments, territories, economies, and relationships. These factions control star systems across the procedurally generated galaxy, establishing borders, conflict zones, and core worlds that define the player's experience. The faction system drives gameplay through reputation mechanics, territorial conflicts, trade opportunities, and narrative context for missions and encounters.

    Factions are defined by FactionData resources (loaded from .tres files in resources/factions/) and managed at runtime by the FM (FactionManager) autoload singleton. Territory allocation uses BFS cellular expansion — deterministic: the same galaxy seed produces identical faction layouts every time.

    Government Types¶

    Each faction has a government type that influences station generation, mission availability, NPC behavior, and economic preferences. The seven types range from democratic republics to lawless anarchy.

    Type Code Description
    Democracy DEMOCRACY (0) Elected officials govern with principles of liberty and mutual defense. Trading bonuses, lawful society. Known for advanced technology and strong navies.
    Oligarchy OLIGARCHY (1) Corporate control by a council of powerful interests. Mining bonuses, pragmatic neutrality. Self-reliant frontier settlements bound by economic cooperation.
    Dictatorship DICTATORSHIP (2) Military-focused authoritarian rule. Combat bonuses, strict law enforcement. Stability maintained through noble governors and military hierarchy.
    Theocracy THEOCRACY (3) Religious order governing spiritual matters. Exploration bonuses, isolationist tendencies. Worship of cosmic mysteries and ancient stellar phenomena.
    Anarchy ANARCHY (4) No central control — lawless frontier. Smuggling bonuses, chaotic internal dynamics. Loose alliances of outcasts operating in unclaimed space.
    Feudal FEUDAL (5) Noble houses with mixed governance styles. Balanced bonuses across military and civilian sectors. Traditional hierarchies adapted to spacefaring society.
    Corporate CORPORATE (6) Company rule driven by profit and trade. Economic focus, mining and trading bonuses. Corporate conglomerates controlling major trade routes.

    Faction Identities¶

    Terran Federation (FED)¶

    • Government: Democracy
    • Colors: Primary #3380E6 (blue), Secondary #E6F2FF (white-blue), Map #408BF2 (light blue, 70% alpha)
    • Territory Weight: 2.5 — vast democratic empire spanning many systems
    • Preferred Economies: Industrial, Manufacturing, Agricultural, High Tech
    • Station Types: Military Outpost, Trading Post, Research Station
    • Commodity Bias: Values data (1.2×), consumables (1.1×), trade goods (1.05×)
    • Policy: Does not allow smuggling
    • Lore: A democratic alliance of human worlds united under principles of liberty, equality, and mutual defense. The Federation maintains the largest standing navy in the galaxy and is known for its advanced technology and strong trading networks.

    Stellar Empire (EMP)¶

    • Government: Dictatorship
    • Colors: Primary #E62626 (red), Secondary #FFD94D (gold), Map #F23333 (light red, 70% alpha)
    • Territory Weight: 3.0 — the largest territorial empire in the galaxy
    • Preferred Economies: Military Industrial, Mining, High Tech, Industrial
    • Station Types: Military Outpost, Research Station, Trading Post
    • Commodity Bias: Values raw materials (1.1×), salvage (1.05×)
    • Policy: Does not allow smuggling
    • Lore: A vast imperial state ruled by an Emperor and a council of noble houses. The Empire values strength, order, and military prowess. While authoritarian, the Empire maintains stability across its worlds through a complex hierarchy of noble governors and military governors.

    Free Merchants Guild (FMG)¶

    • Government: Corporate
    • Colors: Primary #33BF4D (green), Secondary #FFE680 (gold-yellow), Map #40CC59 (light green, 70% alpha)
    • Territory Weight: 1.5 — moderate territorial presence focused on trade hubs
    • Preferred Economies: Trading, Mining, Manufacturing, Industrial, High Tech
    • Station Types: Trading Post, Spaceport, Research Station
    • Commodity Bias: Strongly values trade goods (1.25×), consumables (1.15×)
    • Lore: A corporate conglomerate of trading houses, shipping companies, and merchant princes. The Guild controls major trade routes and maintains neutral relations with most factions. Profit drives their decisions, and they can be found doing business almost anywhere.

    Outer Rim Collective (ORC)¶

    • Government: Oligarchy
    • Colors: Primary #E68026 (orange), Secondary #996633 (brown), Map #F28D33 (light orange, 70% alpha)
    • Territory Weight: 1.8 — substantial frontier territory in the outer reaches
    • Preferred Economies: Mining, Manufacturing, Military Industrial, High Tech
    • Station Types: Military Outpost, Trading Post, Research Station
    • Commodity Bias: Strongly values raw materials (1.2×), salvage (1.1×)
    • Lore: A loose federation of mining colonies, industrial worlds, and frontier settlements in the galaxy's outer reaches. The Collective values self-reliance and mutual aid. Life is hard on the rim, but the Collective provides protection and economic cooperation for its members.

    Pirate Confederation (PIR)¶

    • Government: Anarchy
    • Colors: Primary #661A80 (purple), Secondary #262626 (dark gray), Map #802699 (light purple, 70% alpha)
    • Territory Weight: 0.6 — small, scattered pirate bases in unclaimed space
    • Preferred Economies: Salvage-based economy
    • Station Types: Military Outpost, Spaceport (hidden bases)
    • Commodity Bias: Strongly values artifacts (1.3×), salvage (1.25×), trade goods (1.1×)
    • Policy: Does not allow bounty hunting; pirate faction flag set
    • Lore: A loose alliance of pirate captains, smugglers, and outlaws who have carved out hidden bases in unclaimed space. While fractious and prone to internal conflict, the Confederation presents a unified front against the major powers. They raid shipping lanes and hide in the shadows.

    Church of the Void (CTV)¶

    • Government: Theocracy
    • Colors: Primary #D9D9F2 (pale lavender), Secondary #9966CC (purple), Map #CCCCF2 (light lavender, 70% alpha)
    • Territory Weight: 1.2 — moderate territory focused on isolated research stations
    • Preferred Economies: Military Industrial, High Tech
    • Station Types: Research Station, Trading Post
    • Commodity Bias: Strongly values artifacts (1.25×), data (1.2×)
    • Policy: Does not allow smuggling; exploration-focused faction flag set
    • Lore: A religious order that worships the mysteries of deep space and ancient stellar phenomena. The Church maintains isolated monasteries and research stations, seeking spiritual enlightenment through exploration and study of the cosmos. They are generally neutral in galactic politics.

    Axiom Directorate (AXM)¶

    • Government: Oligarchy
    • Colors: Primary #38E0E6 (cyan), Secondary #BFF2FF (light cyan), Map #2ED1E6 (medium cyan, 70% alpha)
    • Territory Weight: 0.9 — small but strategically important territory
    • Preferred Economies: Military Industrial, High Tech, Trading, Industrial
    • Station Types: Research Station, Observatory, Trading Post
    • Commodity Bias: Strongly values data (1.35×), artifacts (1.2×)
    • Policy: Does not allow smuggling; exploration-focused faction flag set
    • Lore: A classified technocratic research power built around observatories, anomaly laboratories, survey fleets, and tightly controlled data archives. The Directorate treats scientific discovery and deep-space signal analysis as strategic assets, trading advanced instrumentation for access to dangerous knowledge.

    Relations System¶

    Factions maintain relationships with each other on a scale from -1.0 (maximum hostility) to +1.0 (maximum alliance). These relations are static — defined in faction data resources and used during gameplay to determine NPC behavior, mission availability, and territorial conflicts.

    Relation Matrix¶

    FED EMP FMG ORC PIR CTV AXM
    FED — -0.8 +0.6 +0.3 -0.9 +0.1 +0.25
    EMP -0.8 — +0.2 +0.4 -0.9 -0.3 -0.2
    FMG +0.6 +0.2 — +0.7 -0.6 +0.4 +0.2
    ORC +0.3 +0.4 +0.7 — -0.7 +0.2 +0.35
    PIR -0.9 -0.9 -0.6 -0.7 — -0.2 -0.75
    CTV +0.1 -0.3 +0.4 +0.2 -0.2 — +0.45
    AXM +0.25 -0.2 +0.2 +0.35 -0.75 +0.45 —

    Relation Thresholds¶

    • Allied: > 0.5 — factions cooperate, share intelligence, avoid conflict
    • Neutral: -0.5 to +0.5 — standard diplomatic relations, trade possible
    • Hostile: < -0.5 — active antagonism, combat encounters likely

    Key Relationships¶

    • FED ↔ EMP (-0.8): Cold war rivalry — the two largest powers have been in tension for generations
    • FMG ↔ ORC (+0.7): Strong economic partnership — merchants and miners trade heavily
    • PIR vs Everyone (-0.6 to -0.9): Universal enemy — all major factions oppose piracy
    • CTV ↔ AXM (+0.45): Research alliance — both value scientific discovery and share knowledge
    • FED ↔ FMG (+0.6): Strong trade partnership — federation provides security, merchants provide goods

    Player Reputation¶

    Player reputation with each faction is tracked independently on a scale from -100 to +100. Actions in the game world affect standing with specific factions based on what faction controls the system where actions occur and which faction benefits or suffers from those actions.

    Standing Levels¶

    Score Range Standing Color Description
    +75 to +100 Allied Bright green #33CC33 Trusted ally — access to exclusive missions, discounts, friendly NPCs
    +25 to +75 Friendly Green #66B366 Well-regarded — bonus trade prices, some mission access
    -25 to +25 Neutral Gray #B3B3B3 Unknown status — standard interactions, no bonuses or penalties
    -75 to -25 Unfriendly Orange #CC8033 Disliked — higher prices, hostile NPC behavior in some systems
    -100 to -75 Hostile Red #E63333 Enemy — attacked on sight by faction NPCs, denied access to controlled systems

    Reputation Changes¶

    Reputation changes are triggered by: - Contract completion: Success/failure of missions affects the contracting faction's standing (0.12–0.42 per contract) - Combat outcomes: Destroying or sparing faction ships - Trade decisions: Buying/selling with faction-controlled stations - Exploration: Discovering systems in faction territory

    Reputation History¶

    The last 50 reputation changes are tracked with timestamps, faction IDs, delta values, and reasons. This history feeds into news events and narrative feedback for the player.

    Territory & Policy¶

    Territory Generation¶

    Factions expand their territories using BFS cellular expansion from initial seed points. The territory_weight property controls how much territory each faction claims: - High weight (2.5–3.0): Large empires spanning many systems (Federation, Empire) - Medium weight (1.2–1.8): Moderate frontier presence (Collective, Church of the Void) - Low weight (0.6–0.9): Small scattered territories (Pirates, Axiom Directorate)

    Territory Zones¶

    Each territory is divided into three zones that affect gameplay: | Zone | Definition | Gameplay Effect | |------|-----------|-----------------| | Core | Systems at territory center | High security, friendly NPCs, exclusive missions | | Border | Systems adjacent to other factions | Moderate security, potential conflict encounters | | Conflict Zone | 10 systems deep from border | Low security, active faction conflicts, bounty opportunities |

    Policy Flags¶

    Factions have policy flags that affect gameplay: - is_pirate_faction: Marks the faction as pirate — affects NPC behavior and mission types - allows_smuggling: Whether smuggling is permitted in faction territory (only Pirates allow it) - allows_bounty_hunting: Whether bounty hunting contracts are available for this faction - is_exploration_focused: Faction values exploration — provides bonuses for survey missions

    Economy Preferences¶

    Each faction has preferred economy types that influence which systems fall under their control: - FED: Values industrial and high-tech economies (types 5, 6, 7) - EMP: Military-industrial focus with mining (types 4, 8, 7) - FMG: Broad economic interests across trading, manufacturing, and tech (types 1, 2, 3, 6, 7) - ORC: Mining and industrial production (types 2, 3, 4) - PIR: Salvage-based economy (type 0) — operates in lawless space - CTV: Military-industrial and high-tech research (types 5, 7) - AXM: Diverse tech-focused economies (types 4, 5, 6, 7)

    • Drives economy-trade through commodity category biases and preferred economy types
    • Influences npcs-patrol-wings NPC behavior based on player reputation with controlling faction
    • Provides context for discovery-exploration mission generation in faction-controlled systems
